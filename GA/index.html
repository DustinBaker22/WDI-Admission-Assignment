First and foremost, I would like to thank General Assembly for taking the time to give me the opportunity to showcase some of what I can do. The possibility of joining a coding boot camp, in order to jump start my new career within the Web Development community is very exciting, and I honestly cannot wait to dive-in head first. I truly appreciate you giving me this opportunity.

As you already know, my name is Dustin Baker. I am originally from Miami, Florida, and am presently living in San Antonio, Texas. I am 36 years young and I have a 10 year old son who is my world. I am a licensed massage therapist for the state of Texas. The art of computing has been a passion of mine for many years; however, the need to provide for my family made this desire take a back seat in my life. I seized every opportunity I could find to read and learn about developing websites. I taught myself flash and succeeded in building my own photography website. A friend of mine, who works for Udacity, was the person who brought web development to my attention. He opened my eyes to this field and has been helping me understand about web development.

Ever since I decided to look into Web Development, I have really immersed myself within the WebDev community in order to reach my goal to become not only just a front-end or back-end web developer, but a FULL-STACK WEB DEVELOPER! At 36 years of age, I am finally finding what it is that I want to do for a living. Sadly, it has taken me this long to actually listen to all of my friends and family about doing something with computers. I have always been the type of person who had the instinct to fix computers. Whether it is software or hardware based, I am very good at fixing any issue that I come across; and, if I cannot seem to fix it, then Google to the rescue it is!

Five months ago, I decided to start looking into web development. I started with Tree House but then a friend of mine, who was also studying web development, told me about Thinkful. I decided to enroll into Thinkful's 3 month Front-End Web Development course. The course covered HTML, CSS, JQuery, Javascript, and AJAX. Once I was enrolled, I was assigned a mentor that I was only allowed to talk with once a week for only 30 minutes at a time. For me, HTML and CSS was a breeze; however, once I got into JQuery, I found that I needed to get assistance from a mentor. I found it a bit frustrating to only have access to mentoring 30 minutes per week. It is not that I want, or expect anyone to “hold my hand” through any of this process because I am not going to be able to learn that way; however, I would like to have someone who can help me when I am in need of an answer to a question that I am having, and not have to wait in order to keep moving forward. This is the main reason why I would like to join a coding boot camp. I believe that being in a classroom setting, surrounded by instructors who know how the code actually works, as well as being surrounded by likeminded individuals going through the same issues and projects that I am, is definitely the most conducive way to learn how to code.

Last month, I joined a great place called Geekdom. Prior to joining, I attended a lot of meetings (meetups) on web development, many that were based at the Geekdom headquarters here in San Antonio. I decided that it would be a great place to go to do my school work, as well as a great place to network. It has totally paid off! I have been able to work on my Javascript coding through Code Academy as well as network with other likeminded individuals, and the meetups are right there. It has been a great experience, to say the least, and I can only imagine what San Francisco holds for the web development community. I am very excited!

I sincerely hope that I have communicated how extremely serious I am about making this career change and that this is a goal that I am willing to put forth more than just three months effort towards. If there is one thing that excites me about web development is that it is forever changing. I will always be learning because being bored with doing the same thing over and over is definitely not part of this career. I am looking for a D.R.Y. (Don't Repeat Yourself) type of career. I appreciate your time, and patience, reading this and taking the time to talk with me. I hope to be able to have the opportunity to become an alumni of General Assembly!
